About Me

I’m a Software Engineer who enjoys Web development, data science and visualization, and applications that help others.

Currently, I work as a Data Scientist and Software Engineer at Commonwealth Computer Research, Inc. in Charlottesville, VA, where I work on an open-source, distributed, spatio-temporal database, GeoMesa. Previously, I interned at Genworth Financial in Richmond, VA, where I implemented a database-driven CodeIgniter (PHP) webapp to inventory 300+ server nodes, talk to Puppet, and control the provisioning and orchestration of each node through a simple UI. I also served as a Resident Advisor and Senior Resident at the University of Virginia for three years while in school.

Personal projects:

  • I made DidUVaWin.com. Go Hoos.
  • I created a system to organize Fraternity Rush at UVa for my fraternity, Theta Chi.
  • I helped design visualizations of Mumford and Sons lyrics.
  • I designed an application to manage the completion of UVa’s “114 Things To Do Before We Graduate” list.

I made my mark at UVa in being the first Senior Resident for Tuttle-Dunnington Residence Hall, serving as a Class of 2014 Trustee, and working as the Marketing Committee chair for the 2013 Lighting of the Lawn.

In my free time, I listen to and play a lot of music. I love watching comedy, my favorite movies are the Back to the Future trilogy, and I have a giant t-shirt collection (144 and counting). I’m also always up for trying new craft beer.
