About Me

I’m a software engineer focused on frontend web development and data visualization.

Currently, I work as a UI/UX Software Engineer at CCRi., where I work on Stealth, a webapp for performing complex spatial analytics and visualizing geospatial data. Previously, I worked on Arpeggio, a marketing and sales trend analytics application, and GeoMesa, an open-source, distributed, spatio-temporal database.

Recent projects:

  • I participated in the Advent of Code for 2018 using Typescript.
  • I created my own Stranger Things Wall for Halloween, forking an existing repo and adding text messaging to the Upside Down using Google Voice and Python web scraping.
  • I wanted to see Fox news headlines on the president, today vs. 8 years ago.
  • I made DidUVAWin.com. Go Hoos.

Past projects:

  • I created a webapp to automate and organize Fraternity Rush at UVA for my fraternity, Theta Chi.
  • I helped design visualizations of Mumford and Sons lyrics.
  • I developed a webapp and Android application to track students' completion of UVA’s “114 Things To Do Before We Graduate” list.

I graduated from the University of Virginia in 2014. I was the first Senior Resident of Tuttle-Dunnington Residence Hall, served as a Class of 2014 Trustee, and worked as the Marketing Committee chair for the 2013 Lighting of the Lawn.

In my free time, I listen to and play a lot of music. I once starred in a dramatic reenactment true crime show on Discovery Channel, my favorite movies are the Back to the Future trilogy, and I homebrew my own beer. Plus, I’m always up for trying a new craft brew.
